Of Faded Blue Printed Quilts

An ongoing experiment by a person named El.



August 23rd, 2022

Juxtaposition


You're terracota pots waiting to be painted,
A wholly embroidered craft tucked away in a drawer.

You're bright patterned yellow shirts and clean white shoes,
Extra kitchen towels and exactly one bathroom mirror.

You're royal blue fluffy carpets and hard firm mattresses,
Strong bold baby pink beads and subtle muted greys.

You're a lazy-lie-in-bed-do-nothing Tuesday morning,
Yet, a bustling sweet savory Sunday marketplace.

You're the light shade of invisible lipstick you insist is too much,
You're the loud lavender helmet biking downtown.

You're snow storm walks and beach sea glass pickings,
Sleep-in movie evenings and sleep-late midnight talks.

You're stir-fry-calamarai, cool-coffee-tiramisu,
And sticky-melting-chocolate-ice-cream filled hot summer afternoons.

You're young marbled leaves and old pink flowers,
And three-all-in-one different shoots snug in a single clay pot.

You're all the thoughts you spend on others,
All the hours you spend on beef fry,

You're all the tea you never make,
All the things you never say.

You're all the plans I never made,
All the plans I'll always try to make.
