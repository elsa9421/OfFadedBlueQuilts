February 8th, 2022

Something Old, Something New


I'm blocking you out.
Your Tears your Sneers your Years,
Your Wishes your Love your You,
For all the wrong you did Her.
You ask me for something I never had the right to give,
For you gave from what wasn't yours to give,
Promises and Smiles and Lies and Lies.
And you look affronted, you play victim,
But I've seen the darkness,
Stared at it long enough to know how deep it runs.
Leave, out damned spot, you're not wanted now.
In the dead of the night,
Tears scarcely dry,
The birds were asleep but you woke us three,
Three many cooks. But not many, less than less.
Hot coffee in my hands - I didn't feel it's warmth.
Leave, out damned spot, you're not wanted now.
And I see it in me, in how glacial I can be,
In how numb I've become, slush in my veins,
Sitting across you - cold tea in my cup,
And hot, burning tears in yours,
They runneth over. In vain, in vain.
Don't you see what you've turned me into?
And yet you ask. Fester boil.
Pester fester tester.
Doth not blood run thicker than water?
And yet again you ask. How blind. How dull.
How blindingly dull.
And now we come full circle.
Three many cooks. But Three is more, much more than more.
My cup runneth over.
