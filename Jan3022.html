In An Ideal World

In an ideal world we would have never met. In an ideal world there would be no heavy lidded light tracing of limbs in bright, harsh florescence. In an ideal world there would be no half empty disposable bottles littering your apartment's hardwood floors. In an ideal world there would be no 2 a.m steamy showers followed by reheated overly salty morzrella sticks that somehow taste better than if they were done right.

In an ideal world there would be no exploitation, although I manage to joke that's it's exploration vs exploitation and I wonder if you'll ever know that I'm being incredibly, horribly selfish. In an ideal world I wouldn't use a three syllable word when a simpler one would suffice. No walls, no burns, no screams. In an ideal world I would have never learnt that I could scream.

In an ideal world I would never know what you taste like in candle light. In an ideal world you'd never go looking for autumn scented candles

In an ideal world I'd never learn how to finely chop garlic and you'd never know a quicker way to melt butter on a sizzling hot pan. In an ideal world there would be no rehashing of hard learnt lessons from people long since forgotten. No curiosity, no cats, no curious dead cat jokes.

In an ideal world your blinds would never be drawn and light reflecting off the fresh fallen snow would stream into the chaos that is your living room. In an ideal world weekends would be longer and time would stop as snow storms rage through the night. No rushed sleepless evenings, no tangled afternoon naps, no early Monday morning meetings.

In an ideal world I'd never misplace another earring.

In an ideal world I'd stay.
